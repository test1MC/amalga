Terminology concepts

 

This topic describes the concepts related to the Terminology platform application, included with .

In , the Terminology platform application provides services that can be used to store, map, and query clinical terminologies and their related clinical concepts and synonyms, and apply those clinical concepts to data assets by assigning semantic tags based on those concepts.

Working with clinical terminologies

When describing the care and treatment of patients, a structured vocabulary of terms called a clinical terminology is used to consistently and accurately describe complex clinical concepts, such as diseases and treatments, associated with care. Each clinical terminology represents a single domain of clinical concepts, and clinical concepts are typically represented by unique codes within the domain of a given clinical terminology.

For example, the International Classification of Diseases, Ninth Edition (ICD-9) clinical terminology uses standardized codes and display terms to identify and classify clinical concepts. The following table provides just a few of the thousands of display terms and codes supported by the ICD-9 clinical terminology.

Even within a single clinical terminology, multiple display terms can be used to describe the same, single clinical concept. Many clinical terminologies include the idea of a synonym, a display term which maps to another concept. For more information about synonyms, see Synonyms on page 1.

A single provider can use multiple clinical terminologies, and typically must map clinical concepts between multiple clinical terminologies to ensure that incoming data from external sources can be accepted and loaded into the provider's data store. Not all clinical terminologies cover the same domain of clinical concepts, and some clinical terminologies are periodically revised to incorporate changes in the medical field.

The same clinical concepts can be described in very different ways between clinical terminologies. Often, a mapping terminology is used to map clinical concepts between clinical terminologies. Working with semantic tags

In , the terminologies and concepts stored and organized by the Terminology platform application are expressed as semantic tags, strings applied to data assets that provide additional metadata for organization, search, and performance functionality.

Semantic tags can be applied as messages are acquired, shredded, and parsed in the data pipeline, ensuring that clinical concepts are consistently applied to incoming data. Semantic tags can also be applied to specific data assets, to provide discoverability and organization metadata for the storage system in .

For more information about applying semantic tags, see Semantic tags on page 1.
