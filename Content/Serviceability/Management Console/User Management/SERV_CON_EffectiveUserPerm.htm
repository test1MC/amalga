Effective user permissions

 

This topic describes management of roles, lists built-in roles, describes effective user permissions, and discusses computer accounts as related to roles.

The goal of the access control framework is to show the right data to the right user at the right time. By default, the access control framework grants no access to data and applications. Permissions are explicitly granted by assigning user or group accounts to roles and authorizing those roles for data and applications.

A user can be a member of multiple roles. During a user session, considers the entire collection of roles for a user. The user receives the union of all access given to his or her roles; each role receives access to the union of all resources (data and applications) for which it was authorized.

For example, if Dr. Smith belongs to both the Physicians role and the Doctors role, her permissions for data and applications are determined by the union of her role memberships; therefore, if access to sensitive data for emergency room patients is limited to the Doctors role, her membership in that role will allow her to view this data.
