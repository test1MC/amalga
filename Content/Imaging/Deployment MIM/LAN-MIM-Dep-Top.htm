This topic explains the topology for the .

Typically, a care provider organization has multiple health facilities including hospitals, clinics, and other service delivery points, which may be geographically remote from each other. aggregates medical data from different sites and systems by ingesting the HL-7 feeds and storing the text-based data at a central location. Although the network bandwidth between health facilities is often sufficient to enable a centralized approach for HL-7 messages, it may be far from meeting the performance and scalability demands for feeds, each of which can involve transferring gigabytes of data every day.

To overcome this problem, the supports both centralized and distributed deployment topologies.

  • Centralized topology

    Ingestion of feeds happen at a central location, which is typically a data center. In addition to single-facility organizations, care providers with multiple facilities can choose this option provided that feeds are sent from each remote location to the central location.

  • Distributed topology

    feeds are ingested locally at multiple facilities. While the incoming images are stored only locally, part of the ingested metadata is sent from each remote facility to the central site.
