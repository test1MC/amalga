application (IRDA)

 

The application (IRDA) exposes the following two APIs for consumption by controls and applications, including the :

  1. Study, series, instance-level metadata
  2. The name of the corresponding application instance for a given site ID

Typically, a provider works with multiple healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and other service delivery points, which may be geographically remote from each other. collects the medical data from different sites and systems by ingesting and storing the text-based HL-7 feeds at a central location. While the bandwidth between geographically -remote sites may be sufficient to enable a centralized approach for HL-7 messages, it does not meet the scalability demands of data sets, each of which can involve transferring gigabytes of data. Furthermore, the concurrent accesses to centralized data from different sites would have a negative impact on performance.

To overcome these issues, supports a distributed topology: feeds are received and stored locally, while the metadata extracted from headers is stored at a central location, similar to HL-7 feeds.
